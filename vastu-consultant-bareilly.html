Vastu Fundamentals

Key Vastu Guidelines for Homes in Bareilly

Vastu Shastra is an ancient Indian science of spatial design rooted in the directional influence of sun, wind, and natural energy. Here are the core Vastu principles that Bagga Architects applies to every residential project in Bareilly.

🚪

Main Entrance Direction

North, East, or North-East facing entrances are ideal. East-facing entrances receive morning sunlight and fresh energy. North is associated with financial growth. The main door should be the largest and most prominent opening in the house.

🍳

Kitchen Placement

South-East corner is the fire zone and the ideal position for the kitchen. When cooking, the person should ideally face East. North-West is the second-best position. Kitchen in South, West, or North-East creates Vastu doshas that our designs carefully avoid.

🛏️

Master Bedroom

South-West is the best location for the master bedroom — it is the earth element zone associated with stability and grounding. The head of the bed should point South or East. No beam should cross directly over the bed in the sleeping position.

🕉️

Pooja Room

North-East is the divine energy zone and the best location for the pooja room. The person praying should face East or North. No toilet or bedroom should be placed directly above or adjacent to the pooja room. We plan this carefully in every Bareilly home we design.

🪜

Staircase & Toilets

Staircase is best placed in South, West, or South-West. Toilets should be in West or North-West. North-East toilets are a major Vastu dosha that we avoid at the design stage. Staircase should always move clockwise when ascending.

⚖️

Brahmasthana — Centre

The central space of the house is the Brahmasthana — the energy centre. It should be kept light, open, or minimally loaded. Heavy structural columns, walls, or toilets at the exact centre are Vastu doshas we eliminate from floor plans at the design stage.
