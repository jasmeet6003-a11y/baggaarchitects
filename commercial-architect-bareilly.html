Common Questions

Commercial Architect Bareilly — FAQ

  • Commercial buildings in Bareilly require building plan approval from Bareilly Nagar Nigam (BNN) for plots within municipal limits, or Bareilly Development Authority (BDA) for plots in development areas. Required documentation includes site plan with setbacks, floor plans at every level, elevation and section drawings, structural drawings, and Form B signed by a COA-registered architect. For larger commercial projects, fire NOC, environmental clearance, and lift approval may also be required. Bagga Architects handles the entire approval process.

  • Commercial architectural design fees in Bareilly are typically higher than residential fees due to greater complexity, regulatory requirements, and functional specialisation. Fees generally range from ₹20–₹60 per sq ft of total floor area depending on building type, number of floors, and scope of services. A simple shop or office fit-out design starts from ₹20,000–₹50,000. A multi-floor commercial complex or hospital project can range from ₹1 lakh to ₹5 lakhs+ depending on scale. Contact us for a project-specific quote.

Healthcare facility design requires specific knowledge of patient flow, OT design standards, sterile zones, nursing station visibility, ICU layout, medical gas provisions, and accessibility for disabled patients and elderly visitors. We have designed nursing homes and clinics in Bareilly and surrounding districts and are familiar with NABH guidelines and UP state healthcare building norms.

  • This depends on the zoning of your plot under the Bareilly Master Plan or local municipal zones. Mixed-use and commercial development is permitted in specific zones. Converting a residential plot to commercial use requires either a land use change application or compliance with mixed-use norms. Bagga Architects can review your plot's zoning and advise on what is permissible before you commit to a design.

  • Parking requirements under Bareilly municipal building bylaws depend on the type of commercial use and total floor area. Typically, offices require 1 car parking per 50–100 sq m of floor area; shops and showrooms have different norms. Inadequate parking provision is a common reason for building plan rejection in Bareilly. We calculate parking requirements accurately in every commercial design and incorporate them into the site plan from the start.
